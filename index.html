What this harness is for

This is a self-contained playground for the Doseify time-shift engine. The goal is to get the algorithm right before porting it to Swift inside the iOS app. Each scenario you save becomes a regression test fixture: once we port to Swift, the Swift engine should produce the same dose schedule for the same inputs.

What the engine does

Given (home timezone, destination timezone, flight dates, medication schedule, strategy), it produces a day-by-day list of dose times in both UTC and local clocks. It also flags warnings: doses too close together, too far apart, or falling in the configured sleep window.

Strategies

Snap on landing
Take doses at the home-local clock times right up until landing, then immediately take them at the destination-local clock times. Simplest. Best for small shifts (≤3h) or short trips (≤3 days). For large shifts, produces an awkward "swap" dose where two adjacent doses are much closer or further apart than usual.
Gradual pre-shift
Move doses by N minutes per day for K days before departure (configurable). On landing, snap any remaining shift to destination-local time. On return, mirror: pre-shift back over K days before the return flight, snap remainder on landing back home.
Split (half before, half on landing)
Pre-shift only half the total shift before departure, then snap the remaining half on landing. Useful when you can't start pre-shifting early enough to cover the full shift gradually.
Suggest
Engine picks: snap if |shift| ≤ 3h or trip ≤ 3 days; gradual otherwise, with shift-per-day = ceil(total shift / pre-shift window).

Assumptions and limitations (be honest about these)

  • Fixed offsets, no DST mid-trip. The harness uses constant UTC offsets per zone. If DST changes during the trip, results will be off by an hour. Real implementation needs IANA zones.
  • No drug pharmacokinetics. "Safe interval" here is a single min/max per medication. Real drug safety depends on half-life, peak times, food interactions, etc. The harness is geometric, not clinical.
  • Strategy choice is not medical advice. Which strategy is appropriate depends on the medication and the prescriber. The harness shows you what happens; it does not tell you what should happen.
  • Single-leg flights only. No layovers, multi-stop trips, or partial-day arrivals that change zone mid-flight.

Engine public shape (this is what we'll port to Swift)

computeSchedule({
  homeOffset, destOffset,         // hours from UTC
  departureLocal: {date, time},   // home local
  flightHours,
  returnLocal: {date, time},      // dest local
  returnFlightHours,
  meds: [{ name, homeTimes:['08:00','20:00'],
           minIntervalH, maxIntervalH }],
  strategy: 'snap'|'gradual'|'split'|'suggest',
  preShiftDays, shiftMinPerDay,
  postRecoveryDays,
  sleepWindow: [startHour, endHour]
}) => {
  doses: [{ medName, utcMs, homeLocal, destLocal,
            displayLocal, displayOffset, phase,
            scheduledClockMin, shiftFraction, notes }],
  warnings: [{ severity, code, message, dosesAffected }],
  summary: { shiftHours, totalDoses, ... }
}
